Rhubarb Fool

Description:

The "fool" originated in the 15th or 16th century, in England. It is a simple layered dessert that layers tart, fresh fruit with whipped cream.

Traditionally made with gooseberries, this recipe is modified a bit by adding rhubarb for a light, springy taste profile.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 lbs rhubarb, trimmed and sliced 1/2 inch thick (about 4 cups or 1 lb prepped)
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped candied ginger
  • 1/2 vanilla bean, split
  • Pinch of fine sea salt
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 stalk rhubarb (for candied rhubarb strips)
  • 1/2 cup (3.5 ounces) granulated sugar (for candied rhubarb strips
  • 1/2 cup water (for candied rhubarb strips)
