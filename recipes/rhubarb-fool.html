Rhubarb Fool



Description:

The "fool" originated in the 15th or 16th century, in England. It is a simple layered dessert that layers tart, fresh fruit with whipped cream.

Traditionally made with gooseberries, this recipe is modified a bit by adding rhubarb for a light, springy taste profile.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 lbs rhubarb, trimmed and sliced 1/2 inch thick (about 4 cups or 1 lb prepped)
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped candied ginger
  • 1/2 vanilla bean, split
  • Pinch of fine sea salt
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 stalk rhubarb (for candied rhubarb strips)
  • 1/2 cup (3.5 ounces) granulated sugar (for candied rhubarb strips
  • 1/2 cup water (for candied rhubarb strips)

Steps:

  1. Put rhubarb, honey, orange zest and juice, candied ginger, vanilla bean, and salt into a saucepan over Medium heat. Stir to combine, cover and cook, stirring every few minutes for 10 minutes (until the mixture has come to a boil and the rhubarb has softened)
  2. Remove from heat and allow to cool, then remove the vanilla bean.
  3. Transfer the compote to a bowl and refrigerate.
  4. Whip cream and sugar until soft peaks form, either by hand or electric mixer on Medium speed.
  5. Set aside 1/3 cup of compote for garnish, then fold remaining compote into the whipped cream. Spoon the fool into six 1/2-cup glasses or dishes and chill for 1 hour before serving.
  6. Top each dish with the remaining compote before serving.
