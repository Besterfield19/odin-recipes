Gingered Pear and Raspberry Pandowdy

Description:

This recipe is a standard pandowdy with a little added pizzazz from adding candied ginger to the biscuit dough.

The result is warm and a somewhat spicy comfort food staple!

Ingredients:

    Fruit Filling:

  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter, at room temp, for pan
  • 1/2 cup (3.5 ounces) granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp plus 1 tsp cornstarch
  • Pinch of fine sea salt
  • 4 large pears, peeled, cored and sliced (2 lbs prepped)
  • 1 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 dry pint (2 cups or 9 ounces) raspberries, fresh or frozen
  • 1 tbsp cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

    • Biscuit:

  • 1 3/4 cups (8 3/4 ounces) all-purpose flour
  • 3 tbsp plus 1 tbsp (1 3/4 ounces) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 10 tbsp (5 ounces) cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes
  • 1/3 cup (2 ounces) chopped candied ginger
  • 2/3 cup plus 1 tbsp cold buttermilk

Steps:

    Fruit Filling:

  1. Position a rack in the lower third of the oven and preheat the oven to 400 degrees F. Butter a 9" cast-iron skillet or 9" deep-dish pie pan.
  2. To make the fruit filling, rub the sugar, cornstarch and salt together in a large bowl.
  3. Add pears and lemon juice and toss until evenly coated.
  4. Gently fold in the raspberries, then transfer the fruit to the prepared pan.
  5. Distribute butter atop the fruit.

    Biscuit:

  1. To make the biscuit, whisk the flour, 3 tbsp of the sugar, the baking powder, and salt together in a bowl.
  2. Add the butter and toss until evenly coated.
  3. Using your fingertips or a pastry blender, cut in the butter until the size of large peas.
  4. Stir in the candied ginger, then pour in the 2/3 cup buttermilk and stir just until the dry ingredients are moistened. The dough will be crumbly, with large pieces of butter still visible.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly-floured work surface and gently press the dough together, then press it into a 9" circle. Carefully place the dough atop the fruit.
  6. Brush the dough with the 1 tbsp buttermilk, then sprinkle with the remaining 1 tbsp sugar.
  7. Bake in the lower third of the oven for 30 minutes, then turn the oven down to 350 degrees F and bake for an additional 20 minutes or until the pastry is golden and the juices are bubbly and thick.
  8. Allow to cool for 30 minutes before serving.
